Gale Shapley Student-Proposing

Initialization:
→ All students start free and unassigned.
→ Every project and lecturer is unsubscribed (no provisional assignments).

The algorithm iterates over each free student:
  • Each student proposes to their highest ranked project, not yet rejected
    Projects go through all their assignments, keeping the higher preferred students.
  • Here there could be two cases :
    • The proposed projects |assigned| = available slots
      → what is done here is, we go through all assignments of those projects
      and get the least ranked student among them.
      — If the lowest ranked student’s rank is higher than(as in number)
      than the current student who proposed, the least student is removed from
      the projects assigned list and the current student is added. The least ranked
      student is added back to the free students list. The current student is removed from the free students list. — If the student ranks lesser than the least ranked student, then the project is skipped. I.e, the student is rejected
    • The proposed project has space, then the student is accepted by the project. Thereby the size of project’s assigned increases by 1.
This continues until : All students are assigned Or all the projects in the preference list are exhausted
Outcome : Student-optimal: Each student gets the best project they can get in any stable matching.
Projects might get less-preferred students
